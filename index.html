About Weather Readings:

Days on Mars are longer than days on Earth, it takes 24 hours, 39 minutes, or one sol for Mars to rotate all the way around. The date is represented by sols elapsed since the start of the mission, August 5, 2012 for the Curiosity Rover.

Every meteorological reading is taken by the Rover Enviornmental Monitoring Station (REMS). The REMS is just one of the many pieces of science equipment on the Curiosity Rover. These readings are sent back to Earth, the readings don't update continuously, but are instead sent in batches, which is why the latest available weather data might not be extremely current.
